    Who Is Mark Andrews Son?: Born on September 6, 1995, Mark Andrews is an American football tight end.

    As of this writing (Saturday, 18, November 2023), Andrews is a tight end for the Baltimore Ravens of the National Football League (NFL).

    In November 2023, he hit the headlines after he sustained another injury during a week 11 game on Thursday, November 16 against the Cincinnati Bengals.

    Andrews injured his ankle during the first quarter after being tackled by Bengals’ linebacker, Logan Wilson. He was brought back to the locker room for further evaluation.

    After the game, it was announced he had suffered a season-ending ankle injury. On Friday, it was reported that Andrews suffered a cracked fibula and ankle ligament injury.

    Mark Andrews played college football at Oklahoma and was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in the third round (86th overall) of the 2018 NFL Draft.

    Andrews was the fourth tight end drafted in 2018 and was the second tight end drafted by the Ravens, after Hayden Hurst (25th overall).

    In May 2018, Andrews was signed by the Baltimore Ravens to a four-year, $3.45 million contract that includes a signing bonus of $836,660.

    He ended the 2018 season with 34 receptions for 552 yards and 3 touchdowns and also made two of the Ravens longest passing plays that season.

    At the end of the 2019 regular season with the Baltimore Ravens, Andrews was named to his first Pro Bowl for his efforts during the season.

    Andrews started the 2020 season off with five catches for 58 yards and two receiving touchdowns in the Ravens’ 38–6 victory over the Cleveland Browns.

    On September 6, 2021, the Ravens signed Andrews to a four-year, $56 million contract extension including $37.6 million guaranteed

    Andrews began the 2022 season as a full-time starter, finishing the season with 73 receptions for 847 receiving yards and 5 receiving touchdowns in 15 games and starts.
