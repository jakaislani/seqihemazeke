    Will Howard parents-American football quarterback, Will Howard was born on September 24, 2001, in West Chester, Pennsylvania in the United States of America.

    Who are Will Howard’s parents?

    Will Howard was born to Maureen Howard and Bob Howard. He shares the same parents with his three siblings; Tori, Ryan, and Grace.

    Who is Bob Howard?

    Bob Howard is the father of Will Howard. He was born on April 30, 1964, and he is currently 59 years of age. He is an alum of Penn State University, along with his brother Tedd Howard.

    Who is Maureen Howard?

    Maureen Howard is the mother of Will Howard. She was born on December 31, 1970, and she is currently 53 years of age. She is a pediatric physical therapist.

    She earned her master’s degree in physical therapy from Temple University and her bachelor’s degree in biology from the University of Delaware.

    In addition, Maureen works as a social worker and is connected to numerous nonprofit organizations, including St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital and the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention. She was honored with the Blue Hen 17&43 Awards from Delaware, her alma mater, in 2019.

    Will Howard career

    As a senior at Downingtown West High School, Howard passed for 2,543 yards and 27 touchdowns, earning him the title of Maxwell Football Club Pennsylvania Player of the Year.

    In addition to sprinting for 512 yards and 21 touchdowns, Howard concluded his high school career with 5,308 passing yards and 48 touchdown passes.

    In basketball, he was also selected to the All-Area team twice and finished his varsity career with more than 1,000 points.
    Howard was considered a three-star prospect and was offered more than 20 scholarships in all.

    He declined offers from Kansas, Maryland, Minnesota, and Rutgers in favor of playing college football at Kansas State.

    In January 2020, Howard became an early enrollee of the Kansas State Wildcats. Skylar Thompson was the starting quarterback going into his rookie season, but he started the final seven games after Thompson sustained a major injury.

    In addition to rushing for 364 yards and three scores, Howard completed the season with 90 completions on 168 pass attempts for 1,178 yards, eight touchdowns, and ten interceptions.

    After challenging for the starting position during practice as a sophomore, he once again filled in for Thompson. Howard made three starts in his six games of action.

    Howard was the starting quarterback for the Wildcats in spring practice as a junior. Move Although Adrian Martinez had originally planned to redshirt the season, he was eventually selected for the starting lineup heading into 2022.

    After Martinez hurt himself against TCU, Howard took Martinez’s place in the starting lineup for the next game against Oklahoma State, leading the Wildcats on four straight touchdown drives.

    After Martinez had another injury versus Baylor, Howard relieved him and completed 19 of 27 passes for 196 yards and three touchdowns.

    When it became apparent that Martinez’s injury was significant, he was once again Kansas State’s starting quarterback. With seven games played, Howard finished the season with 1,633 passing yards, 14 touchdowns, and four interceptions.

    On November 27, 2023, following his final season, Howard went through the transfer portal. After making an official visit to Columbus, Howard committed to Ohio State University for his senior season on January 4, 2024.
